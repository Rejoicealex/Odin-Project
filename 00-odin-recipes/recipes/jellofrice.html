tomato sauce, rice and chicken

Description

Jollof rice is a vibrant and flavorful West African dish celebrated for its vivid red color and rich taste. It's made by cooking rice in a blend of tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices like ginger, garlic, and chili peppers, which infuse the grains with a deliciously tangy and slightly spicy flavor. Often accompanied by meats such as chicken, beef, or fish, jollof rice is a staple at celebrations and gatherings, loved for its satisfying and comforting qualities.

Ingredients

  • rice
  • tomato
  • onions
  • spices

Steps

    li>Prepare the Ingredients: Gather and measure all ingredients: rice, tomatoes (fresh or paste), onions, garlic, ginger, chili peppers (optional), and any meats or vegetables you plan to include.
  1. Blend or Chop Tomatoes: If using fresh tomatoes, blend them into a smooth puree. Alternatively, you can use tomato paste
  2. Prepare the Base: Heat oil in a large pot or skillet over medium heat. Sauté diced onions until translucent, then add minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped chili peppers (if using). Cook until fragrant.
  3. Add Tomato Paste/Puree: Pour in the blended tomatoes or tomato paste. Stir well and cook the mixture until the raw tomato taste diminishes and the oil begins to separate from the sauce (about 10-15 minutes).
  4. Seasoning: Season the tomato sauce with salt, black pepper, and any additional spices you prefer, such as thyme, curry powder, or bay leaves. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  5. Add Rice: Add washed and drained rice to the pot, stirring to coat it evenly with the tomato sauce.
  6. Add Liquid: Pour in enough water or broth to just cover the rice (typically about 1.5 to 2 times the volume of rice). Stir gently to combine.
  7. Simmer and Cook: Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Allow the rice to simmer gently until it absorbs the liquid and becomes tender, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes.
  8. Optional Additions: If using meats or vegetables (such as chicken, beef, shrimp, peas, or bell peppers), add them during the last 10 minutes of cooking to allow them to cook through and infuse their flavors into the rice.
  9. Final Fluffing: Once the rice is cooked and tender, fluff it gently with a fork to separate the grains and ensure even distribution of flavors.
  10. Serve: Jollof rice can be served hot as a main dish or side dish, garnished with fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro if desired.
