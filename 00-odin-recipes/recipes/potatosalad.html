Potatoes, Cabbage, Egg and Jam

Description

Potato salad is a classic dish enjoyed worldwide, known for its creamy texture and versatility. Typically made with boiled potatoes, which are then cooled and mixed with mayonnaise, mustard, onions, celery, and seasonings such as salt, pepper, and sometimes dill or parsley, potato salad can vary in flavor and ingredients based on regional preferences.

It's often served chilled as a side dish at picnics, barbecues, and family gatherings, where its comforting and tangy taste complements grilled meats or sandwiches. Potato salad is loved for its simplicity and ability to be customized with additional ingredients like eggs, pickles, or bacon, making it a beloved comfort food in many cultures.

Ingredients

  • potatoes
  • mustard
  • onions
  • mayonnaise

Steps

  1. Gather Ingredients:
    • Potatoes (about 2 pounds, preferably waxy varieties like Yukon Gold or red potatoes)
    • eggs (2-3, optional)
    • Celery (2-3 stalks, finely chopped)
    • Red onion (1/2 cup, finely chopped)
    • Pickles (1/2 cup, finely chopped)
    • Fresh parsley (1/4 cup, finely chopped)
    • Mayonnaise (about 1 cup)
    • Dijon mustard (2 tablespoons)
    • Apple cider vinegar (1 tablespoon)
    • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  2. Cook the Potatoes:
    • Peel the potatoes if you prefer, or leave the skins on for a more rustic salad. Cut them into bite-sized chunks.
    • Place the potatoes in a large pot and cover them with cold water. Add a pinch of salt.
    • Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 10-15 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender when pierced with a fork.
    • Drain the potatoes and let them cool slightly. You can speed up the cooling process by spreading them out on a baking sheet.
  3. Prepare the Dressing:
    • In a large bowl, mix together the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, vinegar, and olive oil (if using). Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  4. Combine the Salad:
    • Once the potatoes are cool enough to handle, cut them into smaller pieces if needed.
    • Gently fold the potatoes into the dressing until well coated.
    • Add the chopped celery, red onion, and hard-boiled eggs. Mix carefully to combine everything evenly.
  5. Season and Chill:
    • Taste and adjust seasoning with additional salt and pepper if necessary.
    • Stir in the chopped parsley or dill.
    • Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least an hour to allow the flavors to meld.
  6. Serve:
    • Give the salad a final stir before serving. Garnish with extra herbs if you like.

Feel free to customize your potato salad with other ingredients such as pickles, capers, or bacon bits. Enjoy your homemade potato salad!
