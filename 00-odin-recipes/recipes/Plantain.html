Plantain and Eggs

Description

Plantains are starchy fruits similar in appearance to bananas but with a different flavor and culinary use. They are larger and less sweet than bananas, with a green or yellowish skin that darkens to black as they ripen. Plantains can be prepared in various ways depending on their ripeness: green plantains are typically fried or boiled and have a firm, potato-like texture, while ripe plantains are sweeter and often fried until golden and caramelized, creating a delicious contrast of crispy exterior and soft interior. They are a popular ingredient in many tropical cuisines, prized for their versatility and ability to be used in both savory and sweet dishes.

Ingredients

  • eggs
  • pepper
  • onions
  • planatain

Steps

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Gather and measure all ingredients:
    • Riped plantain
    • onions
    • eggs
    • cooking oil
    • salt and pepper.
  2. Prepare the Plantains: Peel the ripe plantains and cut them into slices, about 1/2 inch thick.
  3. Fry the Plantains:
    • Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat; Fry the plantain slices until they are golden brown on both sides, usually about 2-3 minutes per side
    • Remove the fried plantains from the pan and drain them on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  4. Prepare the Eggs:
    • In the same frying pan (you can use a little more oil if needed), crack the eggs into the pan.
    • Scramble or fry the eggs according to your preference (scrambled eggs are common for this dish).
  5. Season and Serve:
    • Season the eggs with salt and pepper to taste.
    • Serve the fried plantains alongside the scrambled or fried eggs.
  6. Optional Additions:
    • You can add extras such as chopped onions, tomatoes, or herbs to the eggs while cooking for added flavor.
    • Serve with a side of avocado slices or a fresh tomato salsa for extra freshness and flavor.
